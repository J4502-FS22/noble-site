7 Beautiful Lakes in the Twin Cities

The state of Minnesota is also known as the land of 10,000 lakes and when they say 10,000 that is no over estimation. In reality, there are almost 15,000. Here is a list of 7 lakes that are in and around the twin cities for you to enjoy the beautiful Minnesota weather.

Lake Bde Maka Ska

3000 W Bde Maka Ska Pkwy
Minneapolis, MN
55408
Visit Bde Maka Ska's website.

Lake Bde Maka Ska has canoe and kayak rentals, sail boats to adore from afar and best of all its close to some great local restaurants.

Lake of the Isles

2500 Lake of the Isles Parkway E
Minneapolis, MN
55405
Visit Lake of the Isles' website.

Lake of the Isles is situated on the same chain of lakes as Bde Maka Ska, but is situated a tad closer to the cities for picturesque skyline views.

Medicine Lake

1920 W. Medicine Lake Drive
Plymouth, MN
55441
Visit Medicne Lake's website.

Medicine Lake is more towards the Western suburbs of the cities, but it provides plenty of family fun, with parks and water sports.

Lake Minnetonka

4610 County Rd 44
Minnetrista, MN
55364
Visit Lake Minnetonka's website.

Lake Minnetonka is certainly in a busier part of the suburbs, but that’s part of its charm. Feel free to enjoy local restaurants and ice cream at the lake!

Lake Como

1360 Lexington Pkwy N
St. Paul, MN
55103
Visit Lake Como's website.

Lake Como is in a different part of town, but that doesn’t mean it’s any less beautiful. It is very close by the free zoo and amusement park nearby.

Brownie Lake

3201 Wayzata Blvd
Minneapolis, MN
55416
Visit Brownie Lake's website.

Brownie lake is, unfortunately, not made of brownies. However, it does have canoes and plenty of other activities on site.
